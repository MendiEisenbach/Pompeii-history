A City Remembered

Though buried for centuries, Pompeii is far from forgotten. Its rediscovery in the 18th century ignited a fascination that changed archaeology forever. Today, Pompeii stands as a symbol of preservation, tragedy, and the endurance of human culture through time.

"Pompeii speaks to us from silence — a reminder carved in stone and ash." — Cultural Historian

Millions visit the site each year, wandering through time-worn streets and buildings. Scholars continue to uncover new insights. Artists, filmmakers, and writers draw inspiration from its haunting beauty. In preserving Pompeii, we preserve a part of ourselves.

Lasting Influence

In Education

Pompeii is taught in classrooms worldwide, offering a vivid look at daily life in the Roman Empire.

In Art and Media

From operas to modern movies, Pompeii's story continues to inspire dramatic retellings and visual storytelling.

In Preservation Science

Techniques developed at Pompeii shaped modern conservation, influencing how ancient sites are studied and protected.
