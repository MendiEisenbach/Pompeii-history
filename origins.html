Where It All Began

Pompeii, located near the Bay of Naples in Italy, was a thriving Roman city founded around the 7th century BCE. Known for its vibrant markets, detailed mosaics, and luxurious villas, it became a vital cultural and commercial center in the Roman Empire.

"The city was alive, colorful, noisy — and then, in a moment, silenced forever." — Unknown Historian

Positioned in the shadow of Mount Vesuvius, Pompeii flourished for centuries. Its streets were lined with shops, bathhouses, and temples. Life in Pompeii reflected the Roman way — art, leisure, religion, and hierarchy. But nature had other plans.

What made Pompeii important?

Pompeii was located on important trade routes and became known for its agricultural production and craftsmanship.

How big was the city?

Archaeologists estimate the population at around 11,000 people, living in a space of about 170 acres.

What languages did people speak?

Latin was the dominant language, but Greek was also spoken among the educated and elite.
