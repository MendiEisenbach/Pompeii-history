Frozen in Ash

When Mount Vesuvius erupted in 79 CE, it buried Pompeii under meters of volcanic ash. That catastrophe preserved thousands of everyday objects — tools, graffiti, jewelry, food, and even loaves of bread. These relics are windows into Roman life, not as imagined, but as it truly was.

"It is not ruins we excavate, but moments of humanity." — Archaeologist’s Field Journal

Many artifacts were discovered in their original places: wine jugs in taverns, dice in gaming halls, bronze mirrors in dressing rooms. The ash didn't just freeze buildings — it captured life.
